Decision statuses are:

- **CONFIRMED** — approved and binding on the implementation unless the product specification is explicitly revised.
- **DEFERRED WITH GATE** — the decision rule is approved, but its value depends on later evidence or deployment configuration.
- **OUT OF SCOPE** — deliberately excluded from this version.

## Architectural summary

The approved design is one standalone, non-upgradeable contract with:

- fixed notice metadata;
- zero initial supply and binary balances;
- one immutable authority address;
- a dedicated 2-of-3 Safe as the mainnet authority;
- one array-based distribution function;
- strict, atomic recipient validation;
- an immutable cap equal to the final approved recipient-manifest count;
- permanently disabled movement, approvals, permits, and burns;
- explicit, irreversible finalization;
- standard mint-style `Transfer` events and one finalization event;
- no REP interaction, arbitrary external call, proxy, recovery, or intended ETH path.

## Approved decisions

| Decision | Status | Approved decision | Rationale | Rejected alternatives | Security benefit | Trade-off | Later verification gate |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Notice-only purpose | **CONFIRMED** | The contract is a non-economic communication artifact that records successful issuance to addresses in a reviewed REP migration-awareness recipient set. It performs no migration and grants no value, right, or entitlement. | Keeps contract behavior aligned with the communications experiment. | Replacement REP, migrated REP, claim, reward, redemption, governance, staking, trading, wallet-connect, or general airdrop behavior. | Minimizes financial, custody, phishing, and composability risk. | Wallets and recipients may ignore or misunderstand the notice. | Communications and wallet-display reviews must verify that the notice-only meaning remains clear. |
| Metadata | **CONFIRMED** | Name: `Augur REP Migration Notice`; symbol: `REPNOTICE`; decimals: `0`; unit per recipient: `1`; initial supply: `0`. | The descriptive metadata identifies the subject while avoiding replacement-token and reward implications. | Short speculative tickers and names such as `MREP`, `REP2`, or `NEWREP`. | Reduces, but cannot eliminate, misleading presentation. | Wallets may truncate or misrender the metadata, and copies can use identical values. | Exact metadata must be verified in source, bytecode, deployed reads, and the wallet test matrix. |
| Fixed metadata implementation | **CONFIRMED** | Name, symbol, and decimals are fixed contract behavior and must be hardcoded or compiled into the reviewed candidate, not supplied as deployment-time metadata. | Reviewed bytecode should correspond to reviewed user-facing metadata. | Arbitrary constructor metadata, mutable metadata, URI systems, and dynamic branding. | Removes a deployment-misconfiguration and post-deployment mutation class. | Changing metadata requires a new candidate and deployment. | Independent review must compare the approved values with source, build artifacts, and deployed reads. |
| Canonical identity | **CONFIRMED** | Only the deployed contract address verified through official Augur surfaces is canonical. Metadata, logos, ABI shape, and copied source do not authenticate a deployment. | Any third party can copy presentation details. | Treating a name, symbol, logo, interface, or price listing as proof. | Provides a single independently verifiable identity rule. | Requires disciplined publication and correction procedures. | The address may be published only after independent deployment and communications verification. |
| ERC-20-shaped boundary | **CONFIRMED** | Expose familiar metadata, supply, balance, allowance, transfer, transfer-from, and approval selectors for compatibility testing, while explicitly not claiming full ERC-20 behavioral compliance. | Wallets, explorers, indexers, and tools commonly recognize this ABI shape. | A transferable ERC-20, ERC-721, ERC-1155, claim contract, or event-only contract. | Retains a small compatibility surface while forbidding economic movement. | Some tools may misleadingly assume transfers or zero-value calls succeed; visibility is not guaranteed. | ABI-level tests and Sepolia observations must verify actual interface behavior and presentation. |
| Standalone implementation | **CONFIRMED** | Implement explicit state, functions, errors, and events without inheriting OpenZeppelin ERC20, Ownable, Ownable2Step, AccessControl, Pausable, proxy, upgradeable, or generalized token frameworks. | The contract deliberately differs from standard token behavior and has a very small state machine. | Inherited token or administrative frameworks and generalized extension points. | Avoids inherited movement, allowance, ownership, upgrade, and unexpected callable paths. | More behavior is implemented directly and must be reviewed carefully. | Review source and ABI for unexpected inherited or externally callable surface. If no OpenZeppelin code is imported, remove that unused dependency later in a separate reviewed `chore(deps)` commit. |
| Supply and balances | **CONFIRMED** | Construction issues nothing. Each successful recipient receives exactly one unit. Balances remain `0` or `1`, and total supply equals unique successful recipients. `balanceOf(address) == 1` means the authority directly issued one notice to that address. | Binary accounting directly represents successful notice issuance. | Prefunded inventory, fractional units, duplicate issuance, movable balances, burn-adjusted accounting, and a separate `wasNotified` mapping. | Makes balances, supply, calldata, and events straightforward to reconcile. | The historical receipt remains visible permanently. | Unit, fuzz, and invariant tests must prove binary balances and exact supply accounting across action sequences. |
| Movement | **CONFIRMED** | `transfer` always reverts, including for zero value; `transferFrom` always reverts; no externally reachable internal movement path exists. | Movement contradicts the direct-receipt meaning. | Transferable, pausable, operator-mediated, bridgeable, or wrapper-supplied movement. | Prevents redistribution, market use, and movement-based accounting ambiguity. | Some ERC-20-oriented tools may show unusable transfer affordances. | Tests and wallet observations must cover positive and zero-value attempts and all reachable selectors. |
| Approvals and permits | **CONFIRMED** | `approve` always reverts; `allowance` always returns zero; allowance helpers, operator approvals, callbacks, permit, permit nonce, and domain separator are absent. | The notice has no legitimate spender use case. | Standard allowances, permit, meta-transactions, operator approval, and approval callbacks. | Removes spender authority and phishing-sensitive approval or signature flows. | Some interfaces may still offer misleading approval actions. | ABI review, tests, and wallet observations must confirm the absence or failure of every approval path. |
| Burn | **CONFIRMED** | Holder burn, authority burn, `burnFrom`, and burn-to-remove workflows are absent. Users should hide the notice through wallet UI. | Burning would break direct-receipt and total-supply reconciliation. | Holder-controlled or administrative burning. | Preserves permanent binary receipt and exact issuance accounting. | Recipients cannot remove the on-chain balance. | Source, ABI, and tests must confirm that no burn path exists. |
| Distribution entry point | **CONFIRMED** | Use one conceptual privileged operation, `distribute(address[] recipients)`, for one-address canaries and multi-address batches. | A single path avoids divergent authorization, cap, finalization, validation, and event behavior. | Separate single-recipient and batch functions, generic multicalls, and arbitrary mint functions. | Reduces privileged callable surface and inconsistent validation risk. | Even a one-address canary uses array calldata. | Implementation review and tests must confirm that no second issuance path exists. |
| Recipient validation | **CONFIRMED** | Reject an empty array, zero address, duplicate within the call, previously notified address, unauthorized caller, finalized state, batch above the approved maximum, and cap overflow. A valid contract address is technically accepted on-chain. | Invalid input should fail visibly and account classification belongs off-chain. | Silent skipping, idempotent duplicates, `extcodesize` filtering, and contract-side EOA or exchange classification. | Prevents ambiguous issuance and avoids unreliable account-type assumptions. | Off-chain tooling must perform strong deterministic prevalidation. | Tests must cover every condition and the operations pipeline must reproduce the same validation before transaction construction. |
| Atomicity | **CONFIRMED** | Distribution is all-or-nothing. Any invalid recipient reverts the entire call, leaving no persistent balance, supply, or event change. | Atomic state makes manifests and transaction results deterministic. | Partial success, best-effort batches, silent skipping, and successful empty operations. | Prevents hidden manifest divergence and incomplete reconciliation. | One bad recipient blocks a complete batch. | Unit, fuzz, invariant, and reconciliation tests must demonstrate rollback for every failure position. |
| Immutable authority | **CONFIRMED** | One nonzero constructor-supplied immutable authority can distribute before finalization and can finalize. It cannot be transferred, replaced, renounced, delegated, or restored. The deployer receives no implicit privilege. | The contract needs only one narrowly defined administrative address. | Ownable, Ownable2Step, role-based access, pending ownership, handoffs, recovery administrators, separate minter/finalizer roles, and hidden deployer powers. | Removes mutable governance, authority-transfer, and secondary-admin attack surface. | An incorrect, compromised beyond recovery, or permanently unavailable authority requires abandonment or redeployment. | Constructor arguments and authority behavior require independent review and simulation before any deployment gate. |
| Mainnet controller | **CONFIRMED** | The immutable mainnet authority must be a dedicated 2-of-3 Safe with three independently controlled signers, no enabled modules, no custom guard, and a reviewed fallback handler. Signer keys must be securely stored and separately backed up, must not be reused for unrelated systems, and should use hardware-backed storage. Every transaction requires manual review before human signing. The Safe must hold no unrelated assets or protocol positions and only limited operational ETH. Agents may never operate it. | Multiple independent approvals are a conservative control for irreversible capped issuance and finalization. | A single EOA, personal or browser hot wallet, 1-of-1 Safe, shared key, delegated signing service, or Safe with unexplained modules or extensions. | Removes the sole-key authorization path and provides reviewable human approval for privileged transactions. | Signer coordination, signer loss, Safe misconfiguration, and immutable-authority recovery failure remain operational risks. | Exact Safe address, owners, version, chain deployment, fallback handler, signer custody, and transaction simulations are **DEFERRED WITH GATE** and must pass the controller gate before candidate deployment. |
| Deployer separation | **CONFIRMED** | The deployer submits creation; the constructor-supplied Safe is the authority. Deployment grants no hidden privilege and no post-deployment authority handoff exists. | Separates transaction submission from continuing contract control. | Automatic deployer ownership, temporary owner powers, and later authority transfer. | Removes a privileged transition and the risk of forgetting or misexecuting a handoff. | Constructor mistakes cannot be repaired in place. | Deployment review must independently compare the intended Safe with constructor data and deployed immutable state. |
| Immutable issuance cap | **CONFIRMED** | A nonzero immutable `distributionCap` equals the exact number of unique addresses in the final approved production recipient manifest. Every successful call satisfies `totalSupply + recipients.length <= distributionCap`. Reaching the cap does not auto-finalize. | The exact manifest count is the strongest meaningful campaign bound and reconciliation target. | Zero cap, arbitrary upper bound, discretionary margin, mutable cap, and automatic finalization. | Bounds maximum issuance and removes unused authority headroom. | Any material recipient-set change after deployment requires abandonment and redeployment. | The final manifest, uniqueness proof, checksum, count, constructor value, and deployed read must reconcile before deployment. |
| Maximum batch size | **DEFERRED WITH GATE** | The future contract must enforce a compile-time maximum. Select it from worst-case new-recipient gas plus calldata, using no more than 50% of the target block gas limit at the pinned test block and any stricter tooling or Safe limit. | A numeric limit cannot be selected responsibly before candidate measurements. | An unbounded array or a guessed constant. | Preserves transaction and block-gas safety margin. | A conservative bound increases transaction count and operational overhead. | The contract implementer must benchmark one-address, typical, proposed-maximum, duplicate, cap-boundary, and revert scenarios; an independent contract reviewer approves the lower safe bound before implementation-candidate freeze. |
| Explicit finalization | **CONFIRMED** | Only the immutable authority may finalize. Finalization is explicit, one-time, irreversible, and repeated calls revert. It permanently closes issuance, preserves balances, stores no separate timestamp, and leaves the authority readable but powerless. Finalization below cap is technically valid only with a written exception. | Human-approved closure is required even when the cap is reached. | Automatic finalization, unfinalize, emergency mint, recovery distribution, authority clearing, and ownership renunciation as the shutdown mechanism. | Provides one auditable irreversible shutdown state. | Premature finalization cannot be repaired and delayed finalization leaves authority active. | Normal finalization requires every intended batch and transaction confirmed; events, balances, and cumulative supply reconciled; supply matched to the approved manifest unless an exception is approved; no unresolved incident; at least 24 hours after the final normal batch; independent review; and controller approval. Credible controller compromise, manifest misuse, unexpected issuance, incorrect transaction preparation, or another active incident permits immediate emergency finalization. |
| Event policy | **CONFIRMED** | Declare the standard `Transfer(address indexed from, address indexed to, uint256 value)` event and emit `Transfer(address(0), recipient, 1)` once per successful issuance. Emit `DistributionFinalized(address indexed authority, uint256 finalSupply)` once on finalization. | Standard issuance events support common indexing; one custom event captures shutdown. | Duplicate `NoticeDistributed` events, misleading transfer events, and event-only accounting. | Provides a minimal, deterministic reconciliation trail. | Indexers may still infer normal transferability from the standard event. | Tests must verify exact fields, counts, rollback on revert, and final supply. |
| No on-chain batch or manifest identifier | **CONFIRMED** | Do not store or emit an operator-supplied batch identifier, manifest identifier, or manifest hash. | Calldata, transaction hashes, standard events, and external checksummed manifests already identify the operation. | Arbitrary on-chain checksums the contract cannot semantically validate. | Avoids conflicting or incorrectly supplied sources of truth. | Operations must maintain disciplined external records. | Each batch must retain deterministic ordering, recipient-array checksum, transaction hash, decoded-calldata verification, and event reconciliation. |
| Public reads | **CONFIRMED** | Expose only name, symbol, decimals, total supply, balance, zero allowance, immutable authority, finalized state, immutable cap, and the frozen maximum batch size. | These reads are sufficient for recognition and verification. | `wasNotified`, owner, pending owner, role enumeration, metadata URI, recovery, pause, or upgrade state. | Keeps the public surface small and semantically meaningful. | Integrators receive fewer convenience methods. | ABI review must confirm the required reads and absence of rejected surface. |
| No upgrade or recovery | **CONFIRMED** | No proxy, upgrade, delegatecall, recovery administrator, token recovery helper, arbitrary multicall, or mechanism that can reopen issuance. | Finalization and disabled behavior must not be bypassable. | Transparent/UUPS proxies, diamonds, modular executors, rescue functions, and emergency upgrade paths. | Preserves reviewed behavior and irreversible closure. | Defects require abandonment and redeployment rather than in-place repair. | Source, bytecode, static analysis, and independent review must confirm the absence of these paths. |
| No external interactions or intended ETH path | **CONFIRMED** | No REP query, transfer, approval, or custody; no migration call, arbitrary external call, callback, hook, oracle, bridge, payable function, intended receive path, withdrawal, or dependence on ETH balance. Exceptionally forced ETH is inert and permanently unrecoverable. | The notice does not need external state or custody. | On-chain eligibility, REP or migration integration, external executors, recovery, and ETH handling. | Removes reentrancy, dependency, custody, and asset-recovery authority. | Forced ETH cannot be recovered. | Source, bytecode, static analysis, and tests must verify no intended call or value path and no balance-dependent logic. |
| Independent review | **CONFIRMED** | Before mainnet, require independent contract review, independent recipient and operations review, and independent communications review by reviewers other than the relevant authors. Contract review covers source, compiler configuration, dependencies, bytecode, disabled paths, authority, cap, atomicity, finalization, tests, and static analysis. Operations review covers methodology, inputs, snapshot, exclusions, cap derivation, manifests, checksums, calldata, reconciliation, and finalization readiness. | Self-review is insufficient for security-sensitive code, data, transactions, and messaging. | Author-only approval or describing informal review as an audit. | Adds separation of duties across all material failure domains. | Review adds schedule and coordination cost and does not prove absence of defects. | Findings must be resolved or explicitly accepted by maintainers before the corresponding release gate advances. |
| Wallet-display testing | **DEFERRED WITH GATE** | Sepolia testing must cover at least two browser wallets, two mobile wallets, one portfolio tracker, one explorer, one spam-filtering interface, and one manual-import interface. Record automatic visibility, manual import, name and symbol truncation, decimals and balance rendering, transfer and approval affordances, spam classification, price metadata, displayed links, error presentation, and post-finalization behavior. | Exact products should be selected based on relevance near test time. | Assuming recognition from ABI shape or promising wallet visibility. | Exposes misleading affordances, spam treatment, truncation, pricing, and error presentation before mainnet. | The notice may remain invisible or confusing despite correct implementation. | Communications maintainer and deployment operator select the product matrix immediately before Sepolia; recorded results block the testnet gate. |
| Communications policy | **CONFIRMED** | Use the approved notice-only core message, make the official Augur website page canonical, publish a verified address only after independent review, direct users to navigate independently, and prohibit approval, transfer, swap, burn, bridge, claim, or wallet-connect instructions. | Consistent messaging is essential because metadata is copyable and third-party interfaces are untrusted. | Token-attached migration URLs, metadata authentication, promotional value language, or interaction-based calls to action. | Reduces phishing, impersonation, and economic confusion. | Communications cannot prevent fraudulent copies or misleading third-party data. | The canonical page and exact URL are **DEFERRED WITH GATE** and must be approved before public Sepolia communication or mainnet deployment. |

## Accepted architectural trade-offs

The maintainer has explicitly accepted these residual design trade-offs:

| Trade-off | Consequence | Required control |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Immutable-authority unrecoverability | An incorrect, unavailable, or irrecoverably compromised Safe cannot be replaced in place. | Independently verify the Safe and constructor data; abandon or redeploy the candidate when necessary. |
| Exact-cap redeployment risk | A changed final recipient set cannot increase the deployed cap. | Freeze and checksum the reviewed manifest before deployment; redeploy for a material change. |
| Strict-batch operational friction | One invalid recipient reverts the entire submitted batch. | Deterministic prevalidation, batch checksums, simulation, and per-batch reconciliation. |
| Possible wallet invisibility | Correct ABI and events may still be hidden or classified as spam. | Mandatory category-based Sepolia testing and no visibility promises. |
| Fake metadata copies | Third parties can copy all names, symbols, interfaces, and source. | Authenticate only by the independently verified contract address on official Augur surfaces. |
| Inert forced ETH is unrecoverable | Exceptionally forced ETH may remain at the address forever. | Ensure logic never depends on ETH balance and add no withdrawal authority. |

Implementation must follow this record and the approved product specification.
